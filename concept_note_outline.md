# Concept Note Outline: Takealot UX/UI Redesign

## Company Profile
Takealot is a South African online shopping platform that allows users to browse products, compare prices, place orders, pay online and track deliveries. The target audience includes university students, young professionals, working adults, families and small business owners.

## Rationale for Design Changes
The redesign focuses on two usability issues identified in the research: information overload during product search and a complex multi-step checkout process. The Smart Product Discovery solution reduces cognitive load by showing simplified filters, smart suggestions and product comparison. The One-Page Checkout solution reduces repeated navigation by combining address, delivery, payment and order summary into one clear screen.

## SWOT Analysis
Strengths: Strong local brand recognition, large product range, delivery tracking.
Weaknesses: Search results can be overwhelming, checkout can feel lengthy, some mobile controls are small.
Opportunities: Smart filtering, simplified checkout, personalised recommendations, stronger mobile accessibility.
Threats: Competitors with faster checkout, users abandoning carts, decision fatigue during search.

## Design Justification
The redesign applies User-Centered Design by focusing on user goals such as fast product discovery, simple comparison and quick checkout. Hick's Law supports reducing the number of visible choices in search and filters. Fitts's Law supports larger touch targets and clearer primary buttons. Nielsen's heuristics support visible progress, user control and consistent navigation.
