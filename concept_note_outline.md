## 1. Company Profile

Takealot is a South African online shopping platform that allows users to search for products, compare options, place orders, pay online and track deliveries. The app supports users such as university students, young professionals, working adults, parents and small business owners.

## 2. Rationale for Design Changes

The redesign addresses two major usability problems identified in Task 1:

1. Information overload during product search.
2. Complex multi-step checkout process.

The new design introduces smart product discovery, simplified filtering, product comparison and a one-page checkout flow.

## 3. SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- Strong brand recognition in South Africa.
- Wide product variety.
- Existing delivery and tracking infrastructure.

### Weaknesses
- Search results can feel overwhelming.
- Checkout can feel long and repetitive.
- Some controls can be small on mobile screens.

### Opportunities
- Smarter search suggestions.
- Better filtering hierarchy.
- Faster checkout to reduce cart abandonment.
- Improved accessibility through larger tap targets.

### Threats
- Competitors with simpler checkout flows.
- Users abandoning purchases because of too many choices.
- Mobile users expecting fast, low-effort shopping.

## 5. Design Justification

The redesign applies UX/UI principles such as:

- Hick's Law: reducing too many choices through smart filters and suggestions.
- Fitts's Law: improving tap targets and button visibility.
- Nielsen's usability heuristics: clearer system status, user control and consistent navigation.
- User-Centered Design: focusing on the needs of mobile shoppers who want speed, clarity and convenience.
